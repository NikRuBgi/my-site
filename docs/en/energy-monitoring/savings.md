# Savings & Cost Analysis

## Cost Calculation Methods

Dybee offers two methods for translating energy savings into cost savings. Choose the method that matches how your client's tariff structure works.

### Facturation (Invoice) Method

- **Reference cost** = the actual invoice amount for the period
- **Predicted cost** = average unit cost × model-predicted consumption

Use this method when the primary goal is to verify savings against actual billed amounts and the tariff structure is relatively stable.

### Unit Cost Method

- Both reference and predicted costs use a fixed unit price
- Recommended when analyzing both **consumption** and **power demand** separately

!!! tip
    Use the Unit Cost Method when your client pays demand charges. It allows you to separate consumption savings from peak demand savings.

## Power Peak Analysis

Dybee analyzes power demand at **15-minute intervals** to identify peak demand events. This supports:

- Identifying spikes that trigger demand surcharges
- Quantifying the cost impact of demand reduction measures
- Monitoring progress after peak shaving interventions

Power peak analysis is available under **Energy Monitoring → Peak Demand**.

## Portfolio Comparison

The **Portfolio** module shows a comparative view across all buildings you have access to. Metrics available for comparison:

- Energy intensity (kWh/m²)
- Savings percentage vs. baseline
- CO₂ emissions
- Energy Star ratings (where applicable)

## Reporting

Energy monitoring results integrate directly into the dashboard widgets (Performance Tracking, Savings Monitoring, Cumulative Energy) and can be included in formal reports. See [Widget Reference](../dashboards/widgets.md) for energy widget details.
